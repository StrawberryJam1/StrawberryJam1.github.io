Shrimp Tempura Ingredients

  • Cake Flour
  • Egg
  • Ice cold water (Make sure no ice inside)
  • Shrimp/Prawns

Procedure

  1. You first put the cake flour on a fine mesh sieve then dab the side of the sieve to avoid messiness
  2. On a seperate bowl add your egg and ice cold water then mix thoroughly. Add about helf of your cake flour but don't over mix it(Have a bit of clumps in batter)
  3. Cut the tail of the shrimp in half the scrape off the dirt water using your knife. Cut up small incisions to prevent shrimp from curling up
  4. Put the shrimp on the dry cake flour then dust it off. After that, dip it in the batter then fry in hot oil
  5. Cook until shrimp is cooked or tail is red. THEN YOU'RE DONE!

Gyoza Ingredients

  • Ground Pork
  • Cabbage
  • Soy Sauce
  • Nira
  • Shiitake mushrooms
  • Garlic
  • Ginger
  • Sesame oil
  • Sake
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Oil
  • Flour
  • Dumpling wrappers

Procedure

  1. First, combine all your ingredients on a large bowl. Then kneed it with your hands until fully combined
  2. After everything is combined, get your dumpling wrappers and ready a bowl of water. Add about a tablespoon of the filling in the middle of the wrapper
  3. Wet your fingers then trace the wet fingers on the edge of the wrapper. Fold in half and make sure it's tightly sealed
  4. Add oil in a pan then place your gyoza. While the gyoza is frying, put flour in water then pour it into the pan and cover it with a lid so the steam could cook the gyoza as well.
  5. Once Gyoza's bottom is golden brown and cooked, put a plate on the pan then flip the pan and YOU ARE DONE!

We have probably seen this or eaten it once in our lives. If not try it! But this is a famous Japanese dish called Tempura. It's a lightly battered deep fried dish to a light, crispy coating. It can be served as a side dish or as the main course. As a side dish, it is served with Vegetable Tempura. As a main dish it is served by itself with it's special dipping sauce.

Now what is Gyoza? You've probaly eaten it or may have seen it on a menu of a Japanese restaurant. Well, Gyozas are Japanese dumpling that was based off to Chinese potstickers. It is a Japanese dish that have been stuffed with meat, vegetables and seasonings. Gyoza tends to be smaller with thinner skin. This makes it have a crispier texture. It is paired with a "Ponzu" dipping sauce that is filled with flavor.
