Adobo Ingredients

  • Soy Sauce
  • Vinegar
  • Chicken/Pork
  • Garlic
  • Whole Blackpepper corns
  • Bay Leaf
  • Ginger
  • Boiled egg (optional)

Procedure

  1. Clean the chicken and ready the ingredients. Cur the chicken inro an adobo cut after.
  2. Pour oil in a large pan or wok. Then first saute√© the ginger until brown but not burnt then onion and garlic until the garlic and onion browned a bit. After that put the chicken in.
  3. Wait for the chicken to brown then pour the soy sauce in. Let it simmer for a bit before putting in the pepper corns and bayleaf then add water.
  4. After about 30 mins, check if the chicken is tender and cooked. Add the vinegar if it is.
  5. After adding the vinegar, make sure the taste is to your preference
  6. Let it simmer for a bit to cook off the strong vinegary taste. If the chicken is cooked and the sauce is to your liking, YOU ARE FINISH!

Sinigang Ingredients

  • Pork
  • Onion
  • Tomato
  • Radish
  • Green Beans(Sitaw)
  • Ocra
  • Pechay
  • Green Chili
  • Sinigang mix (Tamarind for more authentic flavor)

Procedure

  1. Clean your pork and ready the ingredients. While cleaning the pork, boil water in a pot. Put pork when it's boiling.
  2. Let it boil then throw the water (scum of pork has appeared and needs to be thrown)
  3. Put water again and tenderize the pork. If tender add in your onions, tomato and the sinigang mix.
  4. Let it simmer for a bit then add all your ingredients left and let it cooked (Basically blanching the veggies in soup to soke the flavor)
  5. Add fish sauce if needed. THEN YOU'RE DONE!

If you are not Filipino and you've heard of adobo before; you've probably heard it from your friends who are Filipino Or your family members who have friends that are Filipino; mentioned it to you. But what is Adobo? Adobo is the Philippines ' unofficial national dish. Adobo or "Adobar" in Spanish (which means marinade) is a popular Filipino dish. Meat, Vegetables, and Seafood marinated in soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, bay leaf, and black peppercorns, which are simmered and browned in oil.
